A. objective and scope :

1. what is the precise objective - increase the metrics for the complete product or specific product
2. what is the current baseline metric for the desired flow 
3. what is the exact change in the flow - guessing about causal drivers.
4. who will be the target users - depends on the product , like global or limited to certain cities and markets 
   which platforms are in scope ( for online products ) -- like Android, IOS , Web 
   Are there any segments excluded or not for the specifed target users , like new users or old users ...
5. Do we have the data to monitor the metric and how we will quantify the success metric 
   do we already monitor the logs for the specific metric , is that reliable or not , are we already monitoring success metic with certain guardrail 

B . Unit of Randomization : 

- think at will level of randomization would make sense -- at user level , at demographic level ....
- once decided , consistency matters a lot - 
- for implementation we should have mechanism eg hashing at user level 
- for a large traffice case we can follow ramp up strategy - first implement for 10-20% user once metrics are stable we can scale to desired percentage for correct evaluation 

C . Experiment Allocation Strategy :

- set the Unit of Randomization
- set the traffic allocation if required go for partial allocation 
- mentain the consistency using hashing or other techniques 

D. Primary Metric :

-choose the metric which will get most impacted the experiment and is our target metric 
- it should not be influneced by downstream market place factors 
- apply Intention-to-Treat framework : 
You analyze users based on the group they were assigned to, not based on what they actually did or saw.
If you only analyze users who actually saw or used the feature, you break randomization and introduce bias.
 
E. Secondary Metrics : 

These metrics are decided once the user accepts the success metric - these metrics should be like 
-  not effecting the flow
-  not effecting the business income 
-  depends on the product itself 
- generally 4-5 Secondary metrics are enough
